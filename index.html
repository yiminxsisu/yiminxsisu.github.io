ABOUT  RESEARCH 

Hi there! I’m a fifth-year PhD candidate in Information Studies at the University of Maryland’s College of Information (INFO), where I work with my advisor Dr. Ge Gao.

I am a mixed-methods human-computer interaction (HCI) researcher focusing on AI-mediated communication and human-centered design. My research is informed by social science theories and empirical findings. I investigate how people use language technologies, design novel human-AI interaction mechanisms, and examine their effects on communication experiences and capabilities. My work advances empirical and design knowledge for developing AI technologies that enhance communication and information practices in everyday life and work contexts.

I have studied both information and language sciences prior to starting my PhD. I obtained my MS in Applied Data Science and MA in Linguistic Studies with a graduate certificate in Language Teaching from Syracuse University. I completed my undergraduate studies at Shanghai International Studies University (SISU, 上海外国语大学) with a Bachelor of Management in Information Management and System, BA in English, and a minor in Finance. I also spent a wonderful semester as an international visiting student in management information systems at the University of North Carolina Wilmington during college.

Outside of research, I enjoy arts and crafts. I knit, crochet, sew, embroider, paint, journal, among many other things. I'm from Ganzhou, China (赣州) and have lived in Shanghai, China and Syracuse, NY before moving to Maryland. I can be reached at yxiao[at]umd[dot]edu.
