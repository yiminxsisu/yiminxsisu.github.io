ABOUT

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Information Studies at University of Maryland College of Information Studies (iSchool). My general research areas include human-computer interaction (HCI), computer-mediated communication (CMC), computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), and applied linguistics. I apply mixed methods to understand how people use language to communicate information and coordinate collaborative work in computer-mediated contexts.

Previously, I obtained an M.S. degree in Applied Data Science from Syracuse University School of Information Studies (iSchool), where I worked with Dr. Lu Xiao as a research assistant. I also hold an M.A. in Linguistic Studies and a C.A.S. in Language Teaching: TESOL/TLOTE, both from Syracuse University Department of Languages, Literatures, and Linguistics. I obtained a B.M. in Information Management and Information System and a B.A. (minor) in English from Shanghai International Studies University in Shanghai, China, along with an Intercollegiate Minor Certificate in Finance from Fudan University in Shanghai, China.
