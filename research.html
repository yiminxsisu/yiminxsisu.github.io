Anonymity and persuasion in online communication

In this project, we examined anonymity, participation behavior, and persuasion in the Wikipedia community. Wikipedians disclose varying levels of personal information in their user page. We speculated that a Wikipedian's level of anonymity (or identifiability) is related to their motivation of participation and efforts of contribution in Wikipedia collaborative editting, as well as their perceived credibility and status in the community. We anticipated that these factors would in turn influence a Wikipedian's persuasive power in Wikipedia. By analyzing Wikipedian's user page info, editting history, and Articles for Deletion (AfD) discussion history, we found that Wikipedians who disclose more personal information are more involved in the community and more persuasive in AfD discussions. We called for future work that seek to explain this observed relationship and examine if this observation holds true in other types of online communities.

Xiao, Y., & Xiao, L. (2020). Effects of Anonymity on Comment Persuasiveness in Wikipedia Articles for Deletion Discussions. Proceedings of the Fourth Workshop on Natural Language Processing and Computational Social Science, pp.104–115. [Paper]

Annotated corpus of imperative sentences

In this study, we created an imperative corpus using verbal and textual language data from the TV show The Big Bang Theory and Wikipedia’s Articles for Deletion discussions respectively. We developd a rule (syntax)-based algorithm and trained a set of supervised learning models to classify imperative sentences from the data. All classification were manually validated using a semantically-based imperative annotation guideline in existing literature. Apart from the corpus, which contains 10,700 imperative sentences, we also compared the performance of the rule-based algorithm and that of the supervised learning models and discussed their application on written and verbal language data.

Xiao, Y.*, Slaton, Z. Y.*, & Xiao, L. (2020). TV-AfD: An imperative-annotated corpus from the big bang theory and Wikipedia’s articles for deletion discussions. Proceedings of the 12th Language Resources and Evaluation Conference, pp.6542–6548. (*equal contribution) [paper] [data]
