About me
I am an incoming PhD student in Information Studies at University of Maryland College of Information Studies (iSchool). My general research areas include computer-mediated communication (CMC), computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), human-computer interaction (HCI), and applied linguistics. I apply mixed methods to understand how people communicate information and coordinate collaborative work through language and other communication mediums. I focus mostly on computer-mediated contexts to understand how digital technolgies mediate the communication process, discuss how people adopt and interact with such technologies, and identify future design directions to support human communication.

Like my research, my education also revolves around information and language. I obtained an MS degree in Applied Data Science from Syracuse University School of Information Studies (iSchool), where I worked with Dr. Lu Xiao as a research assistant. I also hold an MA in Linguistic Studies and a CAS in Language Teaching: TESOL/TLOTE, both from Syracuse University Department of Languages, Literatures, and Linguistics. I completed my undergraduate studies at Shanghai International Studies University (SISU), a renowned foreign language university in Shanghai, China, with a BM(anagement) in Information Management and Information System and a BA (minor) in English. I also hold an Intercollegiate Minor Certificate in Finance from Fudan University in Shanghai, China, and was an international visiting student at University of North Carolina Wilmington in Fall 2016.
