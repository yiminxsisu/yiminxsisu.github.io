About me
I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Information Studies at University of Maryland College of Information Studies (iSchool). My general research areas include computer-mediated communication (CMC), computer-supported cooperative work (CSCW), human-computer interaction (HCI), and applied linguistics. I apply mixed methods to understand how people use language or other media to communicate information and coordinate collaborative work in computer-mediated contexts and how to design technology to support this process.

I'm currently located in Syracuse, N.Y. and will be moving to College Park, M.D. in August, 2021. I can be reached at yxiao@umd.edu.

Previous education

Like my research, my education also revolves around information and language. I obtained an M.S. degree in Applied Data Science from Syracuse University School of Information Studies (iSchool), where I worked with Dr. Lu Xiao as a research assistant. I also hold an M.A. in Linguistic Studies and a C.A.S. in Language Teaching: TESOL/TLOTE, both from Syracuse University Department of Languages, Literatures, and Linguistics.

I completed my undergraduate studies at Shanghai International Studies University, a renowned foreign language university in Shanghai, China, with a B.M. in Information Management and Information System and a B.A. (minor) in English. I also hold an Intercollegiate Minor Certificate in Finance from Fudan University in Shanghai, China, and was an international visiting student at University of North Carolina Wilmington in Fall 2016.
